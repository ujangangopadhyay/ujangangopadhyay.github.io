Short Course on Information Geometry

Department of Mathematics

National University of Singapore





This short course is provided by Professor Ting-Kam Leonard Wong of University of Toronto, Canada.

Abstract:

Information geometry is the geometric study of statistical manifolds which are spaces of probability or nonnegative measures. It originated from statistical inference and has been applied in diverse fields including statistical physics and machine learning. This short course is an introduction to information geometry and some of its recent developments. Mathematical prerequisites such as differential geometry and convex analysis will be introduced as needed. In the first part, we will introduce statistical manifolds, divergence functionals and the induced dualistic geometry. In the second part, we will discuss some relations between information geometry and optimal transport.
